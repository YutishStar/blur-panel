§ 01 — The concept

Three moods. One house.

A day in the house moves through three states. Morning fog, afternoon clarity, the long blur of a build night. We design the weeks around them.

i.

Frosted

07:30 — 11:00

The morning is quiet. Pour-overs at the long table. Notebooks before laptops. You read your own code from yesterday like a stranger wrote it. The house breathes slowly.

  • silent hours
  • solo reading
  • longhand planning
ii.

Clear

11:00 — 18:00

The middle is sharp. Pair reviews on the couch. Honest feedback without ceremony. The cohort has each other's terminals open in tabs. Decisions get made in minutes.

  • weekly demos
  • open code reviews
  • founder office hours
iii.

Blur

18:00 — late

The night blurs. A record on, dinner cooked together, then laptops back open. Most of what gets shipped, gets shipped here. By 2 a.m. someone is reading a paper out loud.

  • shared dinners
  • long shipping nights
  • 2 a.m. paper club

§ 03 — A day in the house

The shape of a Tuesday.

  1. 07:30 Light comes up in the kitchen. First coffee. No phones at the table.
  2. 09:00 Silent block. Two hours, doors closed, no Slack.
  3. 11:30 Cohort standup, on the stairs. Three sentences each. What you shipped, what's stuck, what you need.
  4. 13:00 Lunch, cooked in pairs. Rotating. The schedule is on the fridge.
  5. 14:30 Office hours with a founder-in-residence. 15-minute slots. Often runs over. Sometimes runs into the night.
  6. 19:00 Long table dinner. Wine. A guest. The kind of conversation you remember.
  7. 21:00 The long shipping block. This is where most of the cohort's lifetime gets compressed into hours.
  8. 02:00 Paper club, if anyone's awake. Optional. Surprisingly always full.

§ 06 — The fine print

Questions, answered honestly.

Is this an accelerator?

No. We take no equity. We don't write checks. We don't run a demo day for investors. It's a residency — eight weeks of focused company, in a house we've taken the time to make good.

Do I need a company already?

No. About a third of each cohort joins solo, mid-project, or between things. We accept people, not stages.

How selective is it, honestly?

The last cohort had 1,840 applicants for 24 seats. We aren't looking for the most impressive résumé in the room — we're looking for the person the rest of the room will be glad they cooked dinner with.

Can I choose my house?

You can rank your preferences. Match is on a best-fit basis; about 80% of admitted residents get their first choice.

What does the housing cost cover?

Bedroom, utilities, communal meals five nights a week, a fully kitted workspace, and the weekly demo program. Travel is on you, with need-based exceptions.

Who is behind it?

Hacker House Collection is independently funded by its founder and alumni. We are not affiliated with any fund, accelerator, or program.
